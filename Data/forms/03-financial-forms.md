# BC Supreme Court - Financial Forms and Disclosure

## Form F30 - Financial Statement
**Purpose:** Comprehensive financial disclosure — income, expenses, assets, debts. One of the most important forms.

**Required when:** Child support claims, spousal support claims, property/debt division, changing existing support orders. If money is involved, you need Form F30.

**Filing timeline:** Within 30 days of filing claim or response (if support/property at issue). Must update if finances change significantly before trial.

**Key sections:**
1. **Income:** Employment, self-employment, investments, government benefits, pension, other sources. Attach 3 years of tax returns and Notices of Assessment.
2. **Monthly expenses:** Housing, food, transportation, insurance, medical, childcare, personal.
3. **Assets:** Real estate, vehicles, bank accounts, investments, RRSPs, pensions, business interests, personal property.
4. **Debts:** Mortgages, loans, credit cards, lines of credit, other obligations.
5. **Property valuation:** Current value of each asset AND value at date of separation.

**Important rules:** Sworn under oath (false info = perjury). Both parties must exchange. Failure to disclose → court sanctions and negative inferences.

**Supporting documents:** 3 years T1 tax returns, 3 years CRA Notices of Assessment, 3 months pay stubs, corporate financials (if self-employed), bank/investment statements, property tax assessments, mortgage statements.

## Form F31 - Child Support Affidavit
**Purpose:** Specific information for calculating child support under Federal Child Support Guidelines. Works with Form F30.

**When to use:** Any child support claim or application to change child support.

**Key info:** Number/ages of children, parenting time arrangements, both parents' income, special/extraordinary expenses (childcare, medical, extracurricular, post-secondary).

**How child support is calculated:** Based on Federal Child Support Guidelines. Paying parent's income determines base amount from guideline tables. Province of residence determines which table. Special expenses (Section 7) shared proportionally by income. Shared parenting (40%+ with each parent) may result in different calculation.

## Form F20 - Certificate of Pleadings
**Purpose:** Court-generated document certifying current state of pleadings in a divorce case. Required for divorce order.

**Process:** Request from registry when applying for final divorce order.

## Financial Disclosure Timeline
1. Claimant files Form F1 → file Form F30 within 30 days
2. Respondent files Form F4 → file Form F30 within 30 days
3. Exchange supporting financial documents
4. If a party fails to disclose: file Form F6 to compel, ask for adverse inferences, ask for costs

## Property Division in BC (Family Law Act)
- Family property generally divided equally (50/50)
- Includes property acquired during relationship
- Excluded property: pre-relationship property, gifts, inheritances (but increase in value during relationship may be divided)
- Family debt also divided equally
- Court can order unequal division if equal division significantly unfair
- Key dates: relationship start, separation date, trial/agreement date
