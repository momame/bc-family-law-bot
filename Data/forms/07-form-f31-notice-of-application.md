# Form F31 - Notice of Application

## What is Form F31?

Form F31 is the "Notice of Application." It is the form you use to ask the court for specific orders during an ongoing family law case. This is how you bring a matter before a judge for a decision BEFORE the final trial.

Form F31 is one of the most commonly used forms in BC Supreme Court family law cases. Almost every case involves at least one application using Form F31.

## When to Use Form F31

You use Form F31 when you need the court to make a decision on an issue while the case is still ongoing. Common applications include:

- Interim (temporary) parenting time or custody arrangements
- Interim child support
- Interim spousal support
- Orders to prevent the other party from disposing of family property
- Exclusive possession of the family home
- Orders about children's passports or travel
- Orders compelling the other party to provide financial disclosure
- Orders to change an existing interim order
- Orders to set aside a default judgment

## How to File and Serve Form F31

1. Complete Form F31 identifying what orders you are requesting and the reasons
2. Prepare a supporting affidavit (Form F30) setting out the facts and evidence
3. File both forms at the Supreme Court registry
4. Serve the other party with the filed documents

## Service Timelines for Form F31

For a regular application, you must serve the other party at least 8 business days before the hearing date. For urgent applications, you can ask the court for permission to serve on shorter notice. In exceptional circumstances (risk of harm, flight risk), you may apply without notice (ex parte), but this is rare and requires strong justification.

## Getting a Hearing Date

After filing Form F31, you need to get a hearing date from the registry. As of January 2026, civil and family long chambers hearings in BC are booked online using the Court Online Booking System (COBS).

## Form F32 - Application Response

Form F32 is the "Application Response." It is the form you file when someone has served YOU with a Form F31 (Notice of Application).

### When to Use Form F32

You use Form F32 after being served with Form F31. It tells the court your position on the orders being requested.

### Deadline for Filing Form F32

You must file and serve Form F32 at least 4 business days before the scheduled hearing date. If you do not file a response, the court may proceed without hearing your side and may grant the orders the other party requested.

### What to Include in Form F32

- Which of the requested orders you consent to (agree with)
- Which orders you oppose (disagree with)
- What alternative orders you want the court to make
- A supporting affidavit (Form F30) if you have evidence to present
