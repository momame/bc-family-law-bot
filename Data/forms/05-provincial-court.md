# BC Provincial Court - Family Law Forms

## Provincial Court vs Supreme Court — When to Use Which

**Provincial Court handles:**
- Parenting arrangements (guardianship, parenting time, contact)
- Child support
- Spousal support
- Protection orders (family violence)
- Does NOT handle divorce or property/debt division

**Supreme Court handles:**
- Everything Provincial Court handles PLUS:
- Divorce
- Division of family property and debt
- Adoption

**Choosing between courts:**
- If you need a divorce → Supreme Court (only option)
- If you need property division → Supreme Court (only option)
- If you only need parenting or support orders → either court
- Provincial Court is generally faster, less formal, and cheaper
- Supreme Court has more procedural requirements

## Provincial Court Form 1 - Application to Obtain an Order
**Purpose:** The starting form for a family law case in Provincial Court. Equivalent to Form F1 in Supreme Court.

**When to use:** You want parenting arrangements, child support, spousal support, contact orders, or protection orders in Provincial Court.

**Key sections:** Applicant/respondent info, relationship details, children's info, what orders you are asking for.

**Filing:** File at Provincial Court registry. Filing fee is lower than Supreme Court (~$50 for most applications).

## Provincial Court Form 4 - Reply
**Purpose:** Response to an application. Filed by the person who has been served.

**Deadline:** 30 days after service.

## Provincial Court Form 5 - Counterclaim/Application
**Purpose:** Filed when the respondent wants to ask for their own orders in addition to responding.

## Provincial Court Form 6 - Consent Order
**Purpose:** When both parties agree. Equivalent to Form F52 in Supreme Court.

## Provincial Court Form 10 - Application About a Protection Order
**Purpose:** Apply for a protection order due to family violence. Can be done urgently, even without notice to the other party.

**Types of protection orders:**
- No-contact order
- No-go order (stay away from specific locations)
- Conditions on contact (e.g., only written communication)
- Must surrender weapons

**Emergency protection:** Can be obtained quickly, sometimes same day. After-hours applications possible through a justice of the peace.

## Provincial Court Form 8 - Financial Statement
**Purpose:** Financial disclosure in Provincial Court. Similar to Form F30 in Supreme Court but somewhat simpler.

**Required for:** All child support and spousal support applications.

## Moving Between Courts (Transfer)
- You can transfer a case from Provincial Court to Supreme Court (or vice versa) in some circumstances
- Common reason to transfer UP: originally filed only for support in Provincial Court, now also need property division (which only Supreme Court handles)
- Common reason to transfer DOWN: case in Supreme Court is simpler than expected, Provincial Court would be faster and cheaper
- Process: file an application to transfer, court must approve

## Form Cross-Reference: Provincial vs Supreme Court

| Purpose | Provincial Court | Supreme Court |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| Start a case | Form 1 (Application) | Form F1 (Notice of Family Claim) |
| Start jointly | N/A | Form F3 (Joint Family Claim) |
| Respond | Form 4 (Reply) | Form F4 (Response) |
| Counterclaim | Form 5 | Form F5 (Counterclaim) |
| Interim application | Form 1 (new application) | Form F6 (Notice of Application) |
| Respond to application | Form 4 | Form F8 (Application Response) |
| Consent order | Form 6 | Form F52 (Consent Order) |
| Financial disclosure | Form 8 | Form F30 (Financial Statement) |
| Protection order | Form 10 | N/A (use Provincial Court) |
| Divorce | NOT available | Form F1 (with divorce claim) |
| Property division | NOT available | Form F1 (with property claim) |
