# Form F3 - Notice of Family Claim (Starting Your Case)

## What is Form F3?

Form F3 is called "Notice of Family Claim." It is the primary form used to START a family law case in BC Supreme Court. If you are the person initiating the case (the claimant), Form F3 is the first document you file.

Note: On the BC Government website, Form F3 is listed as "Notice of family claim." The direct download link is available at www2.gov.bc.ca under Supreme Court Family Rules forms.

## When to File Form F3

You file Form F3 when you want to ask the court for any of the following:
- A divorce from your spouse
- Division of family property and family debt
- Spousal support (maintenance)
- Orders about children including guardianship, parenting arrangements, parenting time, contact with a child, child support
- A determination of parentage
- Any combination of the above

## Who Files Form F3?

The claimant (the person starting the case) files Form F3. The other party is called the respondent.

## How to File Form F3

1. Complete the form (available as a PDF from the BC government website)
2. File the original plus one copy at any BC Supreme Court registry
3. Pay the filing fee (approximately $200; fee waivers available via Form F86)
4. You will receive a court file number
5. You must then serve the form on the respondent

## Service Requirements for Form F3

After filing, you must personally serve Form F3 on the respondent. This means someone other than you must hand-deliver the documents directly to the respondent. The person who serves the documents must then complete Form F15 (Affidavit of Personal Service) or Form F16 (Affidavit of Ordinary Service).

Service must be completed within 12 months of filing. If you cannot locate the respondent, you may need to apply for substituted service.

## What Happens After Form F3 is Filed and Served?

After being served, the respondent has 30 days to file Form F4 (Response to Family Claim). If the respondent was served outside of Canada, they have 60 days. If the respondent does not file a response, the claimant may apply for default orders.

## Related Forms

- Form F1: Notice of Joint Family Claim (when both parties file together)
- Form F4: Response to Family Claim (respondent's reply)
- Form F5: Counterclaim (respondent wants additional orders)
- Form F8: Financial Statement (required when support or property is claimed)
- Form F15: Affidavit of Personal Service
- Form F16: Affidavit of Ordinary Service

## Form F1 - Notice of Joint Family Claim

Form F1 is used when BOTH parties agree to start a family law case together. This is called a joint family claim. It is commonly used in uncontested divorces where the parties have already agreed on the terms.

### Key Differences Between Form F1 and Form F3

- Form F1 is signed by both parties; Form F3 is signed only by the claimant
- Form F1 does not require personal service because both parties are already participating
- Form F1 typically leads to a faster process
- If one party later wants to withdraw from a joint claim, they must file Form F2 (Notice of Withdrawal from Joint Family Law Case)

### When to Use Form F1 Instead of Form F3

Use Form F1 when both parties agree on what they are asking the court to decide, both parties can cooperate to file together, and the divorce or family matter is largely uncontested.

## Form F2 - Notice of Withdrawal from Joint Family Law Case

If you filed a joint claim using Form F1 but one party wants to withdraw, they file Form F2. After withdrawal, the remaining party can continue the case using the standard contested process.
