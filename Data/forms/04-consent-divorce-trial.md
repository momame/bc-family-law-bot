# BC Supreme Court - Consent Orders, Divorce, and Trial

## Form F52 - Consent Order
**Purpose:** When both parties AGREE on what orders they want, they can submit a consent order without going to trial.

**When to use:** Both parties reached agreement on some or all issues (through negotiation, mediation, or collaborative process). This is the fastest and cheapest way to resolve a family law case.

**Process:**
1. Parties reach agreement
2. Both parties (or their lawyers) draft the consent order using Form F52
3. Both parties sign the form
4. File at the court registry
5. A judge reviews the order without a hearing (desk order)
6. If the judge approves, the order is made and filed
7. If the judge has concerns, they may request changes or set a hearing

**Important for child-related orders:** The judge must be satisfied the consent order is in the best interests of the children. The judge is NOT required to approve just because both parties agree.

**What can be in a consent order:** Parenting arrangements, child support, spousal support, property/debt division, name changes, divorce.

## Form F35 - Requisition
**Purpose:** A multi-purpose form used to request specific registry actions. Used for administrative steps in the court process.

**Common uses:**
- Request a trial date
- Request a case management conference
- Request entry of a default order
- Request a Certificate of Pleadings (Form F20)
- Request the court file be transferred to another registry
- Request other registry actions

## Form F36 - Bill of Costs
**Purpose:** Used to claim costs (legal expenses) from the other party after a court decision.

## Divorce Process in BC Supreme Court (Step by Step)

**Requirements for divorce:**
- Must have been married (common-law partners do not need divorce but can make claims under the Family Law Act)
- Must have been separated for at least 1 year (most common ground) OR adultery OR physical/mental cruelty
- At least one spouse must have been ordinarily resident in BC for at least 1 year
- Arrangements for children must be satisfactory

**Contested divorce process:**
1. File Form F1 (Notice of Family Claim) — includes divorce claim
2. Serve on respondent
3. Respondent files Form F4 (Response) within 30 days
4. Both file Form F30 (Financial Statement) if support/property at issue
5. Attempt resolution through mediation or negotiation
6. If unresolved, file Form F6 for interim orders as needed
7. Attend case management conference (court may order one)
8. Trial preparation conference
9. Trial — judge makes final orders including divorce
10. Divorce Order issued
11. Certificate of Divorce available 31 days after order (appeal period)

**Uncontested divorce process (desk order — no court appearance):**
1. File Form F1 or Form F3 (Joint Family Claim)
2. If F1: serve on respondent, wait for response or default period
3. File Form F30 if support at issue
4. Prepare Divorce Affidavit (contains required information for divorce)
5. File Form F35 (Requisition) to request divorce order
6. Registry prepares Form F20 (Certificate of Pleadings)
7. Judge reviews on paper — desk order (no hearing)
8. Divorce Order granted
9. Certificate of Divorce available 31 days later

**After divorce:**
- Certificate of Divorce is the official proof of divorce
- You can legally remarry 31 days after the Divorce Order
- Support and property orders remain in effect regardless of divorce
- You can apply to vary support orders in the future if circumstances change significantly

## Trial Preparation
**Trial brief:** Written summary of your case, evidence, and legal arguments.
**Witness lists:** Must be filed and served before trial.
**Book of documents:** Organized collection of exhibits for the judge.
**Trial management conference:** Held before trial to organize issues and estimate length.
