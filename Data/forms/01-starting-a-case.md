# BC Supreme Court Family Law Forms - Starting a Case

## Overview
BC Supreme Court handles family law matters including divorce, property division, spousal support, child custody, guardianship, and parenting arrangements. Forms use prefix "F" (Family). All forms available at www2.gov.bc.ca under Justice > Courthouse Services > Documents & Forms > Court Forms > Family Forms.

## Form F1 - Notice of Family Claim
**Purpose:** The form to START a family law case in BC Supreme Court. Filed by the claimant (person initiating the case).

**When to use:** You want a divorce, property/debt division, spousal support, or orders about children (guardianship, parenting time, child support).

**Key sections:** Claimant/respondent info, type of orders requested, marriage details (if divorce), children's names/birthdates/living arrangements, property/debt description.

**Filing:** File original plus one copy at Supreme Court registry. Filing fee ~$200 (fee waivers available). Must serve respondent within 12 months.

**Service:** Must be personally served (hand-delivered by someone other than claimant). Server completes Affidavit of Personal Service. 30-day response period starts.

**After filing:** Receive court file number → serve respondent → respondent has 30 days to file Form F4 → if no response, may proceed by default.

## Form F3 - Notice of Joint Family Claim
**Purpose:** Both parties agree to start a case together. Less adversarial. Common in uncontested divorces.

**Difference from F1:** Both parties sign, no personal service needed, speeds up process significantly.

## Form F4 - Response to Family Claim
**Purpose:** Respondent's formal response after being served with Form F1.

**Deadline:** 30 days after service (60 days if served outside Canada). Late filing may result in default.

**Content:** Indicate which claims you agree/disagree with, your version of facts, orders you want the court to make.

**If you do NOT file F4:** Claimant can apply for default order. Court may grant orders without hearing your side.

## Form F5 - Counterclaim
**Purpose:** Filed by respondent WITH Form F4 when wanting additional orders beyond just responding.

**Example:** Claimant asked for divorce and spousal support. You also want property division not in the original claim.

**Important:** Must be filed at same time as Form F4.

## Workflow: Starting a Contested Case
1. Claimant files Form F1 at registry
2. Claimant serves F1 on respondent personally
3. Respondent has 30 days to file Form F4 (and optionally Form F5)
4. Both parties file Form F30 (Financial Statement) if support or property at issue
5. Parties may attempt mediation or negotiation
6. If agreement reached → Form F52 (Consent Order)
7. If no agreement → Form F6 applications for interim orders
8. Case management conference or trial preparation conference
9. Trial

## Workflow: Starting an Uncontested Divorce
1. Both parties file Form F3 (Joint Family Claim)
2. Both file Form F30 if support/property at issue
3. Wait for divorce requirements (1 year separation)
4. File Form F35 (Requisition) requesting divorce order
5. File Form F36 (Certificate of Divorce) or Form F20 (Certificate of Pleadings)
6. Receive Divorce Order and Certificate of Divorce
