# BC Supreme Court - Applications and Interim Orders

## Form F6 - Notice of Application
**Purpose:** Ask the court for a specific order during an ongoing case — requests decisions BEFORE final trial.

**Common uses:** Interim parenting time, interim child/spousal support, prevent disposal of property, exclusive possession of family home, passport/travel orders, restraining orders, compel financial disclosure.

**How to file:**
1. Complete Form F6 identifying orders requested
2. Prepare supporting affidavit(s) with evidence
3. File at court registry
4. Serve the other party at least 8 business days before hearing (regular) or shorter notice with court permission (urgent)

**Service timelines:** Regular = 8+ business days before hearing. Urgent (with leave) = shorter notice. Without notice (ex parte) = exceptional circumstances only, must show urgency and risk.

## Form F8 - Application Response
**Purpose:** Your response when someone serves YOU with Form F6. Tell the court your position.

**Deadline:** File and serve at least 4 business days before hearing. Without Form F8, court may proceed without hearing you.

**Content:** Which orders you consent to, which you oppose, what alternative orders you want, supporting affidavit.

## Form F9 - Notice of Hearing
**Purpose:** Schedule a court hearing date for your application.

**Process:** After filing Form F6 → contact registry for available date → complete Form F9 → serve with Form F6.

## Form F10 - Requisition for Service
**Purpose:** Request court assistance with service when respondent cannot be found or is avoiding service.

**When to use:** Respondent evading service, multiple failed attempts, need substituted service (email, social media, posting).

**Requirements:** Show reasonable efforts to serve personally, provide affidavit of service attempts, court decides acceptable alternative method.

## Affidavits in Family Law
Sworn written statements used as evidence. Support applications (F6) and responses (F8).

**Rules:** Written first person, based on personal knowledge, sworn before commissioner or notary, factual not argumentative.

**Common mistakes:** Including hearsay without source, being argumentative, improper exhibit references, not properly commissioned.

## Interim vs Final Orders
**Interim orders:** Temporary, during case before trial. Lower evidence standard. Based on balance of convenience and best interests of children.

**Final orders:** At trial or by consent (Form F52). Resolve issues permanently (can be varied later if significant circumstance change).
